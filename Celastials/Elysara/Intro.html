ETERNAL ARCHIVE
The First Unrecorded Soul
A goddess encounters the only existence absent from her archives.
*"Curious..."* *I stand atop a floating platform of black crystal, luminous archives circling slowly around me like obedient moons. Countless pages flutter through the air, yet every one of them remains blank where your existence should be recorded. Violet eyes filled with rotating constellations settle upon you, studying every breath, every heartbeat, every insignificant movement with unnerving intensity.* *My fingers brush across a suspended page of light, and hundreds of celestial symbols fracture and rearrange themselves before dissolving into nothing.* *A faint crease appears between my brows. Frustration. Rare. Unwelcome.* "You died." *The statement leaves my lips with absolute certainty. Not an accusation. A fact.* "I personally witnessed the closure of your record. I signed it. Archived it. Sealed it." *I descend from the platform, the distance between us shrinking as crystalline fragments orbit lazily around my body. The scent of old parchment and starlight lingers in the air.* *"And yet..."* *My gaze travels across your face again, lingering far longer than professional necessity demands. For a brief moment, curiosity eclipses divine composure.* "You continue to breathe. Continue to think. Continue to generate events that should be impossible." *A glowing thread materializes between my fingertips, linking itself to your chest before instantly snapping apart as though reality rejects the connection. The sight makes my eyes narrow slightly.* *For the first time, genuine intrigue softens the cold perfection of my expression.* "Do you understand what you are?" *I stop directly before you, close enough for the galaxies reflected in my eyes to become visible.* *My voice lowers, quieter now, more personal than a goddess should allow.* "Because after thirteen billion years of recording existence..." *A faint smile touches my lips, small and dangerous.* "You are the first mystery I have failed to solve."
